5 Must Know Tips To Keep Your Home Safe

in

Baby

   SHARE 26

Once you bring your baby home, it’s important that you baby proof your home. Your baby needs to be in a safe and secure environment and we know that you love your baby so much that you will ensure that your baby remains safe.

From baby proofing the house to building his room, everything your baby needs has to be perfect! Amidst all this rush, you’re forgetting something really important. Your baby needs to breathe in fresh air and therefore he needs clean and healthy air.

Did you know, the air indoors is 10 times more polluted than the air outdoors. The indoor air  is stale and filled with several pollutants.

Breathing in stale air is unsafe and unhealthy as it can make you sick. You would hate seeing your little one falling sick wouldn’t you? Therefore it is essential that you take appropriate measures to keep your home clean and the air healthy.

Here are 5 essentials that we recommend for keeping a healthy atmosphere in the house:

1.Take off shoes outside

Good habits begin at home! Keeping your footwear outside will keep the dust, dirt and germs at bay. Your shoes come in contact with dirt, muck, insects and what not. Imagine all of these germs and allergens are given a grand entry into your home! To avoid that, it is better to keep your shoes outside or in a footwear rack placed outside the house.

2.Indoor plants are your best friends

A study conducted by NASA concluded that houseplants can exterminate stale air and purify the house. It protects us from toxins like benzene and ammonia.

You could ideally plant pots like Peace Lily, Lady Palm or Broadleaf plants in your indoor space.

3.Vacuuming the carpets and floors

Pollutants and toxins thrive in the little fibres of your carpets and foot mats. The fabric on your sofa too accumulates dust and grime, as it is used by many people in the house. Cleaning carpets and sofas become a cumbersome task. The dust is difficult to remove and get completely rid of.

Ensure that you use a vacuum cleaner which sucks out all the dust and dirt and keeps your home spotlessly clean.

4.Good ventilation at home

Opening the doors of your balcony to have the pleasant breeze in the hall isn’t enough. Corners of your house like the kitchen and bathroom go ignored. Remember to turn on the chimney when you’re cooking. It will help you in removing the smoke, fumes and other unwanted allergens in the air.

In the bathrooms and toilets, turn on the exhaust fans. These fans help to expel the stale air from the room.

5.Set up air purifiers in the house

Air purifiers are a must these days with so much increase in the pollution. You don’t even realize how many harmful chemicals are present in the air you breathe at home.
